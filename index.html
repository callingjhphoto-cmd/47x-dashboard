Macro Context — What This Means for GCC Scores
Oil above $100 is the GCC sweet spot. Government revenues are strong, sovereign deposits are flowing into the banking system, and fiscal expansion continues. However, the elevated DXY (103.8) creates a headwind for non-oil exporters like EGA — their aluminium becomes more expensive in euro and yuan terms. The Fed holding at 4.50% means GCC central banks maintain elevated rates (EIBOR 4.85%), pressuring leveraged real estate developers like DAMAC and Emaar. Gold at $2,184 signals institutional hedging against tail risks (regional conflict, peg pressure). Net assessment: Macro environment is SUPPORTIVE for GCC but bifurcated — oil-linked sectors benefit, rate-sensitive sectors face continued pressure.
