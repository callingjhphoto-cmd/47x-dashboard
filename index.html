IBOR (Interbank Offered Rate)
The rate banks charge each other. EIBOR (Emirates), SAIBOR (Saudi), KIBOR (Kuwait). These directly affect corporate borrowing costs across the GCC.
Fed Funds Rate
The interest rate the US Federal Reserve sets. Because GCC currencies are pegged to the dollar, GCC central banks must follow the Fed. When the Fed raises rates, borrowing gets more expensive across the entire Gulf.
Liquidity
The total amount of money flowing through global financial markets. Michael Howell's CrossBorder Capital tracks this at ~$188.8 trillion (peaked Q3 2025). More liquidity = asset prices rise. Less = they fall.
Transmission
How changes in US monetary policy transmit to GCC economies via the dollar peg. A Fed rate hike in Washington hits mortgage rates in Dubai within days.
Macro Context — What This Means for GCC Scores
Oil above $100 is the GCC sweet spot. Government revenues are strong, sovereign deposits are flowing into the banking system, and fiscal expansion continues. However, an elevated DXY creates a headwind for non-oil exporters like EGA — their aluminium becomes more expensive in euro and yuan terms. Fed rate policy means GCC central banks maintain elevated rates (EIBOR tracks Fed Funds closely via the peg), pressuring leveraged real estate developers like DAMAC and Emaar. Elevated gold prices signal institutional hedging against tail risks (regional conflict, peg pressure). Net assessment: Macro environment is SUPPORTIVE for GCC but bifurcated — oil-linked sectors benefit, rate-sensitive sectors face continued pressure. Current Regime Implications — Deflation (Q1 2026)
What the current deflationary regime means for GCC sovereign fund sector allocation
1.Reduce cyclical exposure. GCC industrial names (EGA, Ma'aden, SABIC) face demand compression as global growth decelerates. Aluminium and petrochemical volumes are particularly sensitive to Chinese manufacturing PMIs, which are trending below 50. Sovereign funds should trim positions or hedge with commodity puts.
2.Accumulate defensive healthcare and infrastructure. GCC healthcare operators (NMC successors, Aster DM, Saudi German) are counter-cyclical with government-backed demand floors. Infrastructure names tied to Vision 2030 / Expo legacy have sovereign payment guarantees that de-risk cash flows during downturns.
3.Build fixed-income duration. With Howell's global liquidity index contracting, GCC central banks will eventually cut rates once the Fed pivots. Locking in current yields on GCC sovereign sukuks (5-6% range) provides both income and capital appreciation when the cycle turns. ADIA and QIA have historically front-run this trade.
4.Build dry powder for the Goldilocks phase. The deflation regime typically lasts 4-8 quarters before transitioning to Goldilocks (per Howell's historical data). Funds that accumulated capital during 2008-09 and 2020 deflation phases generated the strongest vintage returns. Current positioning should prioritise liquidity for future deployment.
